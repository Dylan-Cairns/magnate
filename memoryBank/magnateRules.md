# Magnate Rules

Designer: Cristyn Magnus  
Players: 2  
Length: about 30 minutes  
Materials: Extended Decktet, resource tokens, two d10, one d6

Courts are not used.

## 1. Components

- Deck: Extended Decktet including Pawns, Crowns, and the Excuse. Courts are not used.
- District markers: Four Pawns plus the Excuse (5 total districts).
- Crowns: Used to determine each player’s starting resources.
- Resource tokens: Six kinds, one per suit: Moons, Suns, Waves, Leaves, Wyrms, Knots.
- Dice: Two d10 (income and tax trigger) and one d6 (tax suit).

## 2. Setup

1. Separate Pawns and Crowns from the deck.
2. Place the four Pawns and the Excuse in the middle; each is a district.
3. Shuffle Crowns and deal 3 to each player. Each player takes 1 resource token per Crown they received (3 total), matching the suits shown on the three Crowns.
4. Deal 3 cards to each player.
5. Choose a first player; turns alternate.

## 3. Turn Structure

Each turn, in order:

1. Roll for and collect resources (see 4).
2. Play exactly one card (choose one: develop a new property outright, buy a deed, or sell a card; see 5).
3. Draw one card (end of turn; see 6).

At any time after rolling and before drawing, a player may trade resources with the bank (see 5.E).  
At any time after collecting resources, a player may develop any deed they own (see 5.C).

## 4. Rolling and Income

### 4.A Taxation trigger

- Roll both d10.
- If either d10 shows 1, immediately resolve taxation once, then proceed to income.

Taxation (d6 mapping of suit):
1: Moons  
2: Suns  
3: Waves  
4: Leaves  
5: Wyrms  
6: Knots

Any player with more than one token of the taxed suit discards down to one. Tokens already placed on deeds are not affected. Resolve taxation before collecting resources.

### 4.B Collect resources (based on the higher d10)

- Use the higher of the two d10. If doubles, collect once.

Results:

- 10: Each player gains 1 resource matching each of their Crown cards (3 total).
- 2–9: For each property (per player) with rank equal to the result:
  - If fully developed, gain 1 resource per suit on that property.
  - If only deeded (not complete), gain 1 resource matching one of that property’s suits (player’s choice).
  - If no matching-rank properties or deeds, gain nothing from this step.
- 1: If both d10 show 1, each player gains 1 matching resource for each Ace property they have in play.

## 5. Playing a Card (choose exactly one)

### 5.A Develop a new property outright (complete in one turn)

- Pay resource tokens equal to the card’s rank.
- All tokens must match the card’s suits, and the payment must include at least one token from each suit on the card.
- Placement rules (districts):
  - Place in one of the five districts.
  - If it is your first property in that district, the card must share at least one suit with the district’s Pawn.
  - If you already have a property in that district, the new property must share at least one suit with the immediately previous property in that district.
  - In the Excuse district, your first property may be any card; subsequent cards must still match the immediately previous property.
  - Once placed, a property cannot be moved. If there is no legal district, you cannot develop it now.
- Ace: Developing an Ace costs 3 tokens that match the Ace’s suit.
- Restriction: You may not develop a new property in a district where you have a deeded but incomplete property. Finish that deed first.

### 5.B Purchase a deed (develop over time)

- Cost: 2 tokens, one per suit shown on the card, paid to the bank.
- Place the deed following the same placement rules as developed properties.
- While a deed exists in a district, you cannot place another deed or develop another new property there until this deed is fully developed.
- You may spend resources to partially develop a deed on the same turn you buy it.
- Buying a deed adds 2 to the total cost to complete the property compared to outright development. Not recommended for rank 2 (same total cost as outright).
- Ace deed: costs 1 token matching the Ace’s suit.

### 5.C Develop properties (later turns)

- After collecting resources, you may spend tokens that match one of the card’s suits to advance a deed.
- Place spent tokens on the deed.
- When the total placed equals the card’s rank, the property becomes fully developed.
- Tokens on deeds are safe from taxation.

### 5.D Sell a card

- Discard a card from hand; gain 2 tokens, one per suit on that card.
- If you discard an Ace, gain two tokens matching that Ace’s suit.
- Mandatory one-card play per turn:
  - You cannot sell a card on the same turn that you develop outright or buy a deed.
  - If you cannot legally develop or deed any card, you must sell one.

### 5.E Trade (bank)

- After resolving die rolls and before drawing, you may trade with the bank:
  - 3 of any one suit → 1 of any other suit.
  - Multiple trades allowed.

## 6. Draw a Card and End of Turn

- Draw 1 card; your turn ends.
- First time the draw pile is exhausted: shuffle the discard pile to form a new draw pile.
- Second time the draw pile is exhausted: each player takes one final turn; then the game ends.

## 7. Victory

At game end:

1. Discard the cards remaining in your hand (you should have two left).
2. Discard incomplete deeds and any tokens on them.

District scoring:

- For each district, each player totals the ranks of their developed properties there.
- An Ace counts as +1 for each property in that district that has the Ace’s suit.
- The player with the higher total in a district scores 1 victory point.
- If tied in a district, no one scores that district.
- The player with more districts (victory points) wins.

Tiebreakers, in order:

1. If tied in districts: compare the sum of ranks of developed properties across all districts; higher total wins.
2. If tied in total properties: the player with more remaining resource tokens wins.
3. If still tied: the game is a draw.
